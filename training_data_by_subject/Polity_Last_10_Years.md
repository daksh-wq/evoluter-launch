### Question [2021]
**[TAGS]**
- **Target Exam:** UPSC CSE
- **Year:** 2021
- **Subject:** Polity
- **Topic:** Constitutional Bodies
- **Difficulty:** Hard

**[QUESTION]**
Consider the following statements:
1. In India, the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) can inquire into complaints regarding violation of human rights only if the complaint is lodged within one year of the incident.
2. The NHRC is not empowered to inquire into any matter if it is already being looked into by a State Human Rights Commission.
Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

**[OPTIONS]**
A. 1 only
B. 2 only
C. Both 1 and 2
D. Neither 1 nor 2

**[CORRECT_ANSWER]**
3

**[EXPLANATION]**
The NHRC does not have a strict time limit of one year for lodging complaints. It can consider complaints even after one year, depending on the circumstances. The NHRC can inquire into matters already being looked into by a State Human Rights Commission, particularly if it deems the matter to be of significant national importance or if the State Commission is not handling it effectively. Therefore, both statements are incorrect.
